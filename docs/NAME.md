# Why "Turul"?

The **Turul** is a mythical bird of prey from ancient Hungarian and Turkic mythology — a divine falcon said to soar above the world, seeing everything below with extraordinary clarity.

## The Connection

A cloud resource analyzer does exactly what the Turul does: it rises above the complexity of your infrastructure and gives you a clear, panoramic view of everything happening across your cloud accounts.

| Turul (the myth) | Turul (the app) |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Flies above all terrain | Operates across AWS and GCP |
| Legendary vision — sees everything below | Scans 200+ cloud services, surfaces hidden costs and risks |
| Guardian and protector | Security analysis, compliance checks, IAM audits |
| Guides its people to safety | Well-Architected reviews, optimization recommendations |
| Symbol of power and oversight | Multi-account, multi-project visibility from one place |

## Why It Fits

- **Altitude** — The Turul doesn't live in the weeds. It sees the whole picture. That's the core value of this tool: a single pane of glass across providers, accounts, and dimensions (cost, security, reliability, compliance, IAM).

- **Clarity** — The bird's defining trait is its vision. Turul turns raw API data from hundreds of services into scores, grades, diagrams, and actionable recommendations.

- **Independence** — The Turul is a wild creature, not domesticated. The app runs entirely on your desktop with no SaaS dependency. Your credentials and data never leave your machine.

- **Guardianship** — In mythology, the Turul watches over and protects. The app continuously monitors for security misconfigurations, overly permissive IAM policies, compliance gaps, and cost waste.

## Pronunciation

**TOO-rule** (rhymes with "pool" with a T and an R).

## Origin

The Turul appears in the origin myths of the Magyar (Hungarian) people, depicted on coats of arms, monuments, and the Hungarian Royal Sword. In Turkic tradition, it represents divine authority and far-seeing wisdom. The largest bird statue in Europe — the Turul monument in Tatabanya, Hungary — has a 15-meter wingspan.
